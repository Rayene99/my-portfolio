If I had a dollar for every training session that started with "Visual, Auditory, or Kinesthetic learners," I'd... well, I'd be training more teachers to ***unlearn this***.

Here's the uncomfortable truth: **Learning Styles are a preference, not a prescription**

The science is clear, and it's been clear for years. Teaching to a student's "preferred" learning style doesn't improve outcomes. Yet, this myth persists in classrooms worldwide, consuming time and resources that could go toward what actually works!

So what does work?

✅ **Dual Coding:** Pairing words with visuals (NOT JUST for visual learners).

✅ **Multimodal Instruction:** Using varied methods for EVERYONE to engage with content differently.

✅ **Retrieval Practice:** Testing and spacing, regardless of how information was presented.

The shift isn't about accommodating individual learning "types." It's about *designing instruction that leverages how all brains actually learn* through multiple pathways, repeated exposure, and active retrieval.
