When I worked on a project to create a new curriculum for the advanced CEFR levels, I handed my team a plan and said: *"pick whatever lesson you want to work on"*. That's it. No assignment. No "you take unit 3, you take unit 5."

Their reaction surprised me; they looked at me like I'd offered them something unusual. Because apparently, being asked*"what you want to work on?"* is not how most of them had experienced before.

Many teachers had been assigned work their whole careers. **Never consulted. Never trusted to have an opinion.**

As teacher trainers, our job is to develop teachers before managing them. And you can't develop someone you don't trust.

Giving people a choice isn't a luxury. **It's the baseline**. 

**If your teachers are disengaged, ask yourself:** *"Have you tried listening to them before bossing?"*
