tagline: An honest, no-fluff survival guide for online teachers, covering
  everything from setup and lesson structure to burnout, boundaries, and the
  emotional realities no training course prepares you for.
summary: This is a comprehensive survival guide for online teachers, written to
  cover both the practical mechanics and the unspoken emotional reality of the
  job. It starts with physical and tech setup and a pre-lesson ritual, then
  breaks down how to teach different age groups from young learners through
  senior adults. It walks through the anatomy of an effective online lesson
  using a reimagined PPP framework, online-specific classroom management and
  presence, and underrated topics like screen fatigue, silence, isolation, and
  cultural competency. It profiles common student types (camera-off, ghost,
  overly dependent), covers what new teachers should expect from themselves
  including imposter syndrome and the comparison trap, and closes with
  engagement strategies, assessment methods, burnout prevention, essential
  tools, and a 90-day survival roadmap.
problem: Most online teaching training focuses narrowly on lesson mechanics and
  platform features while leaving new teachers unprepared for the real,
  day-to-day experience of the job — the silence after asking a question, the
  isolation of teaching alone through a screen, the emotional labor of
  performing energy for young learners, and the burnout that comes from poor
  boundaries. Teachers are rarely told what to expect from students who go
  camera-off or ghost, or from themselves in a rough first month, leaving many
  to think their struggles are personal failures rather than a normal part of
  the transition to online teaching. This guide closes that gap by pairing
  practical classroom strategy with an honest account of the emotional and
  logistical realities of teaching online.
takeaways:

  - Classroom management strategies specific to online teaching, including
    presence, handling disruption, and setting a social contract
  - "How to handle the emotional labor of online teaching: screen fatigue,
    managing silence, isolation, and staying consistent"
  - How to recognize and respond to common student types, including camera-off,
    ghost, and overly dependent students
  - What to realistically expect from yourself as a new online teacher,
    including imposter syndrome, a rough first month, and the comparison trap
  - Practical engagement strategies, including creating low-stress stakes and
    using chat as a teaching tool
  - How to run formative and summative assessment in an online-specific format
  - How to protect your energy, recognize online-specific burnout risks, and
    build a sustainable teaching schedule
  - Which core tools and tech actually matter, plus a 90-day roadmap and
    non-negotiables for surviving your first months
