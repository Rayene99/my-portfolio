tagline: Practical strategies for navigating, resolving, and learning from
  workplace disagreements in educational institutions.
summary: This is a 10-chapter practitioner's handbook (plus quick reference and
  interactive self-assessment) for educators, trainers, and institutional
  leaders dealing with real workplace conflict. It moves from foundational
  theory (types of conflict, the Thomas-Kilmann conflict styles with an
  interactive quiz) through core skills (active listening, Non-Violent
  Communication, reframing, Harvard principled negotiation, BATNA) to applied
  practice (in-the-moment de-escalation with the STOP technique, mediation
  processes, five real institutional case scenarios, documentation of resolution
  agreements, and building a psychologically safe, conflict-resilient culture
  long-term). It closes with a quick-reference guide and ready-to-use scripts
  for common conflict conversations.
problem: "Educational institutions are unusually conflict-prone — high-stakes,
  values-driven work, complex teacher–administrator power dynamics,
  multicultural teams, and resource scarcity all create friction. Most of this
  conflict isn't the loud, obvious kind; it's the quiet kind that builds through
  avoidance: feedback never given, disagreements never named, tension left to
  harden into resentment. Educators and leaders are rarely trained in the
  specific communication and negotiation skills needed to handle disagreement
  productively, so conflict either gets suppressed (damaging trust and
  performance over time) or mishandled in the moment (damaging relationships
  instantly). This handbook gives them a field-tested, practical alternative to
  both."
