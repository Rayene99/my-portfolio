tagline: A ready-to-use encyclopedia of speaking, listening, reading, and
  writing activities for online English classes, grounded in language
  acquisition theory and adapted specifically for the screen.
summary: This is a reference encyclopedia of classroom-ready activities for
  online English teachers, built on a foundation of how the brain learns
  language, the affective filter and language freeze, Bloom's Taxonomy, and
  why good activities flop when they lack real context. It moves through a
  full library of speaking, listening, reading, and writing activities, each
  with instructions, an online adaptation, level/age adaptations, and notes
  on why the activity works linguistically. It closes with practical
  teaching chapters on engagement tactics, differentiation, motivation and
  discipline, assessment and feedback, synchronous versus asynchronous
  activities, screen fatigue, cultural considerations, platform-specific
  guidance, using AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini for lesson
  planning, EdTech tools such as Kahoot, Padlet, and Wordwall, and a
  troubleshooting guide for common online teaching disasters.
problem: Online English teachers often reach for the same handful of
  worn-out activities because most activity banks either ignore why an
  activity works linguistically or were written for in-person classrooms
  and don't translate to a screen, leaving teachers to improvise online
  adaptations on the fly or default to passive, low-engagement tasks. Fewer
  resources connect activity choice back to language acquisition theory —
  the affective filter, cognitive load, Bloom's Taxonomy — so teachers can
  tell why an activity flops instead of just that it did. This encyclopedia
  closes that gap by pairing a theoretical foundation with a large,
  organized library of speaking, listening, reading, and writing activities,
  each built specifically for the online classroom with level, age, and
  platform adaptations.
takeaways:
  - How the brain learns language, and how the affective filter, anxiety,
    and language freeze affect online learners
  - Why good activities flop when they provide content without real
    context, and how to apply Bloom's Taxonomy in activity design
  - A library of speaking activities with step-by-step instructions, online
    adaptations, and level-specific variations
  - A library of listening activities with instructions, linguistic value,
    and tips for making them work online
  - A library of reading activities with instructions, online adaptations,
    and adaptations for lower levels
  - A library of writing activities with instructions, purpose, linguistic
    focus, and level/age adaptations
  - Engagement tactics and differentiation strategies suited to the online
    classroom
  - How to handle motivation and discipline, and how to structure
    assessment and feedback online
  - When to use synchronous versus asynchronous activities, and how to
    manage screen fatigue and cultural considerations
  - Platform-specific guidance for tools like Zoom, Google Meet, and
    Microsoft Teams
  - How to use AI tools such as ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini for lesson
    planning and activity design
  - How to use EdTech engagement tools including Kahoot, Gimkit, Padlet,
    Jamboard/FigJam, Mentimeter, Flip, and Wordwall
  - How to troubleshoot common online teaching disasters, with a
    quick-reference index of key topics
