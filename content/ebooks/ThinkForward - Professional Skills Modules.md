tagline: An interactive, module-based skills program that builds critical
  thinking, sound decision-making, deep listening, structured problem solving,
  and emotional intelligence through practical frameworks and tracked progress.
summary: This is a progress-tracked, module-based professional skills program
  covering six core areas. It opens with critical thinking, using the Paul-Elder
  framework, core habits, cognitive bias, and evidence evaluation via the SIFT
  method. It moves into decision making, covering decision types, the DECIDE
  model, the 10/10/10 rule, pre-mortems, and traps like analysis paralysis and
  groupthink. It covers deep listening through the five levels of listening, the
  LADDER technique, and listening in high-stakes or emotionally elevated
  situations. It then teaches structured problem solving using the 5 Whys,
  SCAMPER, design thinking, and force field analysis, before closing with
  emotional intelligence — Goleman's five components, the amygdala hijack, the
  STOP technique, empathy in practice, assertive communication, the SBI feedback
  model, and navigating difficult conversations.
problem: Professional skills like critical thinking, decision-making, and
  emotional intelligence are often taught as abstract concepts or personality
  traits rather than as learnable, structured techniques, leaving people unable
  to translate the idea of "think critically" or "be more empathetic" into
  something they can actually practice. Training in these areas is frequently
  scattered across unrelated courses, missing the frameworks needed to apply the
  skill under real pressure — in a hard conversation, a high-stakes decision, or
  a moment of elevated emotion. This module series closes that gap by pairing
  each soft skill with named, repeatable frameworks and techniques that turn
  abstract competencies into concrete practice.
takeaways:
  - The Paul-Elder critical thinking framework and the four core habits of
    critical thinkers
  - How to recognize five high-impact cognitive biases and surface hidden
    assumptions
  - How to evaluate evidence using the SIFT method, an evidence hierarchy, and
    common logical fallacies
  - How to distinguish reversible from irreversible decisions and know when to
    trust intuition versus analysis
  - Decision-making frameworks including the DECIDE model, the 10/10/10 rule,
    and the pre-mortem technique
  - How to avoid decision traps like analysis paralysis, groupthink, loss
    aversion, and outcome bias
  - The five levels of listening and why listening is harder than it looks
  - Deep listening techniques including the LADDER technique and reflective
    listening responses, including in high-stakes situations
  - How to define the real problem using the 5 Whys and problem reframing
  - How to generate creative solutions with SCAMPER and design thinking, and
    evaluate them with the Three-Lens Filter and Force Field Analysis
  - Goleman's five components of emotional intelligence and how to recognize an
    amygdala hijack
  - How to build emotional vocabulary, use the STOP technique, and identify
    emotional triggers
  - How to practice empathy, communicate assertively, and give and receive
    feedback using the SBI model
  - How to navigate difficult conversations using the "And Stance" and
    in-the-moment de-escalation techniques
