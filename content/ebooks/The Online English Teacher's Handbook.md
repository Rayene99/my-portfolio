tagline: A rigorous, methodology-driven handbook that equips online English
  teachers with the pedagogy, engagement strategies, and assessment tools to
  design real virtual language learning, not just deliver it.
summary: This is a comprehensive professional handbook for online English
  language teachers, structured around eight core areas. It begins with the
  foundations of online teaching mindset and understanding digital learner
  motivation, then covers setting up a virtual classroom and platforms. It
  presents core ELT methodologies adapted for online spaces — Communicative
  Language Teaching, Task-Based Language Teaching, the Flipped Classroom,
  Blended Learning, Project-Based Learning, and CLIL — each with practical
  online application. It details synchronous and asynchronous engagement
  strategies including breakout room best practices and energisers, teaching
  across age groups and levels, formative and summative assessment, a feedback
  quality ladder from evaluative to dialogic feedback, differentiation and
  scaffolding for inclusion, virtual classroom management and norms, and closes
  with teacher wellbeing, sustainable practice, and continuous professional
  development.
problem: Many online English teachers are trained in traditional classroom
  methodology or given platform tutorials, but rarely shown how to actually
  translate core ELT approaches like task-based or project-based learning into a
  virtual setting, leaving them to either directly port in-person techniques
  that don't work on screen or default to passive, teacher-led delivery instead
  of real learner-centered design. Guidance on engagement, feedback, and
  classroom management is often generic and not adapted to the specific dynamics
  of breakout rooms, asynchronous work, and screen-based attention. This
  handbook closes that gap by pairing established language teaching methodology
  with concrete, online-specific practices for design, engagement, assessment,
  and inclusion.
takeaways:
  - How to shift from being a content deliverer to a learning designer in the
    online classroom, and what motivates digital learners
  - How to set up a virtual classroom, choose platforms, and configure a
    physical setup that supports teaching
  - How to apply Communicative Language Teaching principles in virtual spaces
  - How to run a full Task-Based Language Teaching cycle online, including
    pre-task, task cycle, planning and report, and language focus
  - How to use the Flipped Classroom and Blended Learning models with the right
    digital tools
  - How to design and run an online Project-Based Learning unit, from driving
    question through research, creation, presentation, and reflection
  - How to apply CLIL (Content and Language Integrated Learning) in an online
    context
  - Synchronous engagement strategies, including strong lesson openings,
    breakout room best practices, and energisers/brain breaks
  - Asynchronous engagement strategies and a curated set of digital tools for
    online ELT
  - How teaching approaches shift across young learners, teenagers, adults, and
    advanced learners online
  - How to run formative and summative assessment online, correct errors
    effectively, and climb the feedback quality ladder from evaluative to
    dialogic feedback
  - How to differentiate instruction, scaffold learning, and meet diverse needs
    for inclusion online
  - How to set virtual classroom norms, manage participation, and handle
    technical difficulties
  - How to avoid screen fatigue and burnout, sustain a healthy teaching
    practice, build a digital resource library, and pursue ongoing professional
    development
