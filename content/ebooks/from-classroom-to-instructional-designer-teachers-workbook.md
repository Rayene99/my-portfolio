summary: An interactive, self-paced workbook that walks classroom teachers
  through a structured 5-part plan for transitioning into instructional design
  careers — mapping the field, translating teaching experience into ID language,
  building a portfolio, rewriting a resume, and applying with confidence.
  Includes fillable worksheets, reflection prompts, a 30-day action plan, a
  LinkedIn optimization guide, a 20-term glossary, and a downloadable "Exit
  Pass" summary the user can save as their personal action plan. Answers are
  entered directly in the browser and saved locally — nothing is stored
  externally.
problem: "Teachers who want out of the classroom often have the exact skills
  instructional design employers are looking for (curriculum design,
  learner-centered planning, assessment, stakeholder communication) but don't
  know it, because nobody has ever translated \"lesson planning\" into the
  vocabulary that gets past a resume screen. This creates three specific
  blockers: (1) not knowing which of the many \"instructional design\" job
  titles actually fits them, (2) not being able to reframe teaching experience
  in language that reads as relevant/qualified to a non-education hiring
  manager, and (3) not having a portfolio, since teaching doesn't naturally
  produce one. This workbook removes all three blockers with a concrete,
  do-it-yourself process instead of vague encouragement."
