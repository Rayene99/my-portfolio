tagline: Six two-hour sessions to take English teachers from "never touched AI"
  to a full portfolio of AI-enhanced classroom tools.
summary: A six-week, entry-level professional development programme (one 2-hour
  session per week) that introduces English language teachers to AI and
  technology integration, moving from foundational AI literacy through prompt
  engineering, AI as a teaching assistant, technology-enhanced assessment, and
  multimodal activity design. The instructional design blends experiential,
  problem-based, project-based, inquiry-based, constructivist, and
  self-regulated learning across the six sessions, each with clear learning
  objectives, content, activities, and a portfolio-building assessment task.
  Participants finish with a full professional portfolio (prompt bank,
  AI-assisted lesson plan, rubric with annotated AI feedback, two
  technology-enhanced activities) and a personalised Professional Action Plan
  for continued practice. Includes a full instructional-design table, a
  programme facilitator/designer checklist, and a curated tool and resource
  list.
problem: Many English language teachers are entry-level technology users who
  feel left behind by AI in education but have no accessible, structured on-ramp
  — existing training tends to be either too generic (not ELT-specific) or too
  advanced (assumes prior AI fluency). This creates hesitancy, misuse, or
  avoidance of tools that could meaningfully reduce planning and marking
  workload while improving differentiation for different English proficiency
  levels (A1–C2). This programme solves that with a short, low-barrier, entirely
  hands-on sequence purpose-built for language teaching — CEFR-level prompt
  design, differentiated lesson content, writing feedback, and activity design —
  so teachers leave with usable classroom materials, not just conceptual
  awareness.
outcome: >
  Foundational AI literacy: what AI, machine learning, NLP, and generative AI
  are, and their relevance to English language teaching

  The CLEAR prompt framework (Context, Language, Expectations, Audience, Role) and how to write and refine prompts for different CEFR levels (A1–C2)

  How to use AI as a co-planning teaching assistant — generating lesson plans, differentiated materials, texts, dialogues, and vocabulary/grammar exercises — while maintaining teacher judgment and authority

  How to design formative and summative AI-supported assessments: quizzes, rubrics, and diagnostic tools

  How to generate and critically edit AI-written student feedback (grammar, coherence, vocabulary, style, tone)

  How to design technology-enhanced learning activities mapped to Bloom's Taxonomy, using tools like Padlet, Nearpod, Edpuzzle, and Canva, including multimodal tasks like digital storytelling and podcast scripts

  Ethical considerations specific to classroom AI use: bias, privacy, and academic integrity

  A completed Prompt Bank, an AI-assisted lesson plan with rationale, an AI-generated rubric with critically annotated feedback, and a two-activity technology-enhanced portfolio

  A personalised Professional Action Plan for sustaining AI/tech integration after the programme ends

  A curated toolkit of AI and interactive classroom platforms (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grammarly, MagicSchool AI, Nearpod, Kahoot!, Padlet, Edpuzzle, Canva) organized by teaching purpose
