tagline: Turning new hires into confident online teachers in 8 weeks.
summary: "An 8-week, 4-unit professional development program (~40 contact hours)
  built to onboard novice online English teachers (0–2 years' experience) who
  work with young learners. It blends synchronous live sessions with
  asynchronous self-directed tasks, workshops, case studies, role-play, and peer
  teaching across four units: online pedagogy and lesson design, technology and
  digital-tool proficiency (Zoom, Google Meet, Nearpod, Kahoot!, Padlet, and
  more), virtual classroom management, and online assessment and parent
  communication. The program includes a full curriculum alignment map, a graded
  assessment and weighting structure, and clear certificate-of-completion
  requirements — making it a ready-to-run, evaluator-ready training package
  rather than a loose set of workshop ideas."
problem: Novice teachers moving into online instruction with young learners are
  often thrown in without structured preparation — they know English pedagogy
  but lack the virtual-specific skills to keep young students engaged on screen,
  manage disruptions without a physical room, use ed-tech tools fluently, or
  communicate progress clearly to parents. This leads to inconsistent lesson
  quality, high early-career burnout, and uneven learning outcomes for students.
  This program solves that by systematically building pedagogical,
  technological, classroom-management, and assessment competencies in a
  scaffolded sequence — each unit producing a real, evaluated artifact (lesson
  plans, a live demo, a management plan, an assessment tool) so teachers leave
  certified with a portfolio of practiced, feedback-tested skills rather than
  just theoretical knowledge.
outcome: >-
  Design and submit three interactive, developmentally appropriate lesson plans
  for young learners

  Demonstrate proficiency in three major online teaching platforms and five+ digital tools

  Deliver a live 15-minute online teaching demonstration with strong engagement and pacing

  Create and present a disruption-management plan for common virtual classroom challenges

  Develop, implement, and evaluate an online assessment tool with a sample group

  Communicate student progress to parents clearly and empathetically via practiced role-play scenarios

  Meet certification requirements: 80%+ live-session attendance, all major assessments submitted, 70%+ overall score, and active peer participation
