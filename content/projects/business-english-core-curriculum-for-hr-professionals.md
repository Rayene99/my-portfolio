tagline: A 30-session B1–B2 curriculum that turns HR professionals into
  confident communicators — in English, on the job.
summary: "A complete 30-session, 45-hour Business English program built
  specifically for HR professionals — recruiters, HR business partners, L&D
  officers, and generalists — working at B1–B2 level in English-medium or
  partially English-medium environments. The curriculum is organized into six
  thematic modules (Foundations, Written Communication, Meetings &
  Collaboration, HR-Specific Communication, Presentations, and
  Negotiation/Persuasion/Intercultural Competence), each built on a consistent
  five-stage lesson cycle: Warm-up, Input, Guided Practice, Production, and
  Wrap-up & Assessment. Every session includes full objectives, CEFR-aligned
  can-do outcomes, materials lists, step-by-step teaching procedures, and
  built-in B1/B2 differentiation strategies, making it immediately teachable by
  any instructor — not just its designer."
problem: "Generic Business English courses teach broad workplace language but
  rarely map onto the specific communication tasks HR practitioners actually
  perform — writing job ads, running behavioral interviews, delivering feedback
  using SBI structure, managing grievances, or mediating conflict. HR
  professionals in mixed-level, multinational teams need language training
  that's functional and role-specific, not abstract grammar practice. This
  curriculum closes that gap: it's ESP (English for Specific Purposes) built
  from the ground up around real HR communication genres, with differentiation
  baked into every session so mixed B1/B2 cohorts can be taught together without
  a second parallel syllabus."
outcome: >-
  Communicate confidently in routine and increasingly complex professional
  exchanges

  Write coherent, well-structured business correspondence and HR documentation (emails, memos, reports, policies)

  Contribute to and lead meetings, including drafting agendas and minutes

  Perform core HR tasks accurately in English — interviewing, feedback delivery, policy explanation, grievance handling

  Deliver a structured, audience-aware presentation and field live Q&A

  Negotiate, persuade, and mediate workplace conflict using diplomatic, culturally sensitive language
