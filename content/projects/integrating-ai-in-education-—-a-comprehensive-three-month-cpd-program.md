tagline: A 12-week, research-backed roadmap for turning AI curiosity into
  confident, ethical classroom practice.
summary: A comprehensive, 12-week (3-month) blended-learning CPD program that
  takes educators from AI uncertainty to confident, ethical, and
  pedagogically-sound AI integration. Structured across three progressive months
  — Foundations, Practical Integration, and Mastery & Sustainability — the
  program is built on a multi-framework instructional design methodology (ADDIE,
  Bloom's Taxonomy, UDL, TPACK, Connectivism, 70-20-10, Constructivism,
  Andragogy). Each of the 12 weeks includes learning objectives, topics,
  hands-on activities, curated tool resources, trainer tips, common-challenge
  guidance, and a weekly summative assignment that feeds into a final Capstone
  AI Integration Portfolio. Also includes a full assessment framework, a
  competency/content strand map, an extended activities bank, a troubleshooting
  guide, prompt-engineering templates, and a glossary of AI terms for educators.
problem: 'Most AI-in-education professional development is one-off,
  tool-focused, and disconnected from actual classroom practice — a single
  workshop on "using ChatGPT" that leaves teachers with a list of apps but no
  framework for judgment, no plan for sustained adoption, and no way to address
  the real anxieties (job security, ethics, bias, student misuse, equity of
  access) that block genuine buy-in. This program solves that by treating AI
  adoption as a instructional-design problem in its own right: it sequences
  learning from literacy → application → leadership over 12 weeks, builds in
  critical evaluation and ethics at every stage (not just as an add-on), and
  produces concrete, reusable classroom artifacts (policies, materials,
  assessments, action plans) rather than abstract awareness.'
outcome: >
  Core AI literacy: what AI/ML/LLMs/generative AI actually are, and how to
  evaluate tools using a structured pedagogical framework

  Practical AI-assisted workflows for lesson and unit planning, using the RACE prompt framework (Role, Action, Context, Expectation)

  How to create full teaching materials packs (worksheets, texts, presentations, visuals) and differentiate them for tiered/ELL/SEN learners

  How to design AI-assisted assessments, rubrics, and personalized feedback — plus how to build AI-resistant assessment tasks

  How to design interactive, AI-enhanced, engagement-driven lessons for students

  Critical and ethical AI use: bias detection, academic integrity policy design, data privacy (GDPR/FERPA), and equity auditing

  How to use AI for administrative efficiency (parent communications, report comments, meeting summaries)

  How to lead AI adoption at a school level: building an AI-ready culture, mentoring colleagues, engaging parents, forming a professional learning community

  A finished Classroom AI Use Policy, a student-facing AI literacy mini-lesson, an AI Integration Action Plan, and a complete Capstone AI Integration Portfolio documenting their 12-week growth

  A reusable library of ready-to-use prompt templates, a glossary of AI terms, and a curated resource/tool directory for ongoing learning
