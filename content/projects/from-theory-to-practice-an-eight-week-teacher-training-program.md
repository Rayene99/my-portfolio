tagline: Where classroom theory becomes daily practice.
summary: An eight-week, full-day-per-week professional development program for
  EFL and content-area teaching teams, built around the Model–Observe–Reflect
  (MOR) cycle. Each week follows an identical structure — a trainer-modeled
  demonstration, a peer observation phase using shared calibrated tools, and a
  structured reflection that ends in one actionable micro-goal. The program
  moves from communicative language teaching and task design (Weeks 1–3), into
  data-driven, non-evaluative classroom observation and coaching (Weeks 4–5),
  through human-centric classroom management and teacher wellbeing (Weeks 6–7),
  and closes with responsible AI use and LMS integration (Week 8). Six
  appendices provide reusable tools — an observation checklist, a micro-goal
  tracker, the E-A-S debrief protocol, AI use guidelines, an LMS comparison
  table, and an information-gap activity bank.
problem: 'Most teacher PD is delivered as one-off theory sessions that never
  translate into changed classroom behavior, or as top-down evaluative
  observation that teachers experience as judgment rather than growth. This
  program addresses both failures at once: it embeds professional learning
  directly into the act of teaching itself (rather than lecturing about it),
  replaces evaluative feedback with descriptive, evidence-based peer
  observation, and prevents "feedback overload" by requiring every debrief to
  end in exactly one implementable next step instead of a long list of
  suggestions. It also closes a specific gap in most programs — the absence of
  any structured guidance on teacher emotional sustainability and on
  responsible, privacy-conscious AI/LMS use in the classroom.'
outcome: >-
  Teachers can design communicative, information-gap, and TBL tasks with genuine
  non-linguistic outcomes across proficiency levels (A1–B2)

  Teachers can conduct non-evaluative, descriptive classroom observations using a shared, calibrated Look-For Checklist

  Teachers can run a complete E-A-S (Evidence–Analysis–Suggested Micro-Goal) post-observation debrief that produces one teacher-owned, next-lesson-implementable goal

  Teachers can distinguish surface compliance from genuine cognitive engagement and apply real-time pacing adjustments, including planned "breathing moments"

  Teachers develop concrete boundary-setting and emotional-regulation strategies for sustainable, long-term practice

  Teachers can write CRAFT-framework AI prompts and evaluate AI output for accuracy, appropriateness, and pedagogical value

  Teachers can select and justify LMS features (Google Classroom, Canvas, etc.) with a clear pedagogical rationale, grounded in explicit data-privacy guidelines

  A completed eight-week Micro-Goal Tracker showing a documented trail of implemented classroom changes, evidence, and outcomes
