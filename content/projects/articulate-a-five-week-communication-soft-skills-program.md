tagline: Fluency isn't linguistic — it's human. A one-on-one curriculum for
  adult English learners who already speak the language but want to master the
  art of using it.
summary: ARTICULATE is a fully-developed five-week, 15-session curriculum for
  facilitating one-on-one conversation-based coaching with
  intermediate-to-advanced adult English learners. Built on ADDIE, Backward
  Design, and a blend of Communicative Language Teaching and Dogme ELT, the
  course strips away grammar drills and vocabulary lists in favor of authentic
  dialogue. Each week moves through a thematic arc — self, relationships, work,
  ideas, and vision — with full session plans (warm-ups, discussion questions,
  signature activities, and observation notes), a facilitator's guide, a
  non-graded soft-skills assessment framework, and a curated resource bank of
  books, articles, and TED Talks.
problem: "Many intermediate-to-advanced English learners have already cleared
  the grammar-and-vocabulary hurdle, but still can't tell their story with
  confidence, hold their ground in a disagreement, give hard feedback
  gracefully, or speak persuasively about something they care about. Traditional
  English courses aren't built to address this gap — they keep testing
  linguistic accuracy long after that's stopped being the real barrier.
  ARTICULATE addresses the actual bottleneck: the social, emotional, and
  intellectual skills that make communication genuinely effective, not just
  grammatically correct."
outcome: >-
  Self-Awareness & Expression — articulating identity and personal narrative
  with clarity

  Active Listening — staying present and responding thoughtfully in dialogue

  Empathy & Emotional Intelligence — taking perspective and reading emotional context

  Critical Thinking & Argumentation — forming, defending, and revising a position

  Professional Communication — adapting register, giving/receiving feedback, self-positioning

  Confidence & Authentic Voice — speaking with conviction and personal authority


  Growth is tracked via a non-graded observation rubric and culminates in a capstone "personal manifesto" session synthesizing all six skills.
