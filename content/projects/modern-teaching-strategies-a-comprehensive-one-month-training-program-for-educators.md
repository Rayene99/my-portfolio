tagline: "From transmission to transformation: 20 sessions to reflective,

summary: "A four-week, 20-session teacher training program (approx. 35–40 hours)
  built on a hybrid instructional design model combining ADDIE, Understanding by
  Design, Andragogy, Kolb's Experiential Learning Cycle, and Schon's Reflective
  Practice. The program moves from theoretical foundations (Week 1: learning
  theories, teacher identity, reflective practice) through differentiation and
  inclusion (Week 2: Tomlinson's DI model, accommodation vs. modification, UDL)
  to active learning strategies (Week 3: PBL, cooperative learning, flipped
  classrooms) and closes with assessment and feedback (Week 4:
  formative/summative design, rubrics, Hattie & Timperley's feedback model).
  Each week includes four content sessions plus a hands-on Workshop Day, and the
  program culminates in a Capstone Action Research project. Five appendices
  provide a needs-analysis survey, an observation checklist, a lesson plan
  template, and structured reflection prompts."
problem: Conventional teacher PD tends to deliver strategies as isolated tricks
  disconnected from any coherent philosophy, and rarely builds in structured
  practice, peer feedback, or professional reflection — so new strategies are
  heard once and rarely implemented. This program addresses that by pairing
  every concept with hands-on design work using teachers' own curriculum
  materials, embedding a repeatable experiential cycle (do → reflect →
  conceptualize → apply) into every single session, and requiring teachers to
  leave each week with something concretely usable in their next lesson rather
  than just new knowledge. It also solves the common failure of PD "not
  sticking" by building in built-in accountability structures (SMART goals, peer
  partners, a capstone Action Research inquiry) designed to sustain growth after
  the program ends.
outcome: >-
  Teachers can articulate a coherent, evidence-informed personal teaching
  philosophy

  Teachers can design differentiated lessons using Tomlinson's model and UDL principles, addressing learner readiness, interest, and profile

  Teachers can correctly distinguish and apply accommodation, modification, and scaffolding strategies without lowering expectations

  Teachers can facilitate active learning using PBL, Kagan cooperative structures, and technology integration (SAMR model)

  Teachers can design valid, aligned formative and summative assessments, including criterion-referenced analytic rubrics

  Teachers can write specific, descriptive, growth-oriented feedback calibrated to Hattie & Timperley's four-level model

  Teachers complete a short-cycle PDSA Action Research design to continue inquiry-driven growth beyond the program

  A completed portfolio: Teaching Philosophy statement, differentiated lesson design, exit ticket sequence, analytic rubric, and a documented Teaching Transformation Commitment
