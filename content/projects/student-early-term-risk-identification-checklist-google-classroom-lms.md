tagline: Catch at-risk students in week 3, not week 13.
summary: A structured, four-part diagnostic tool for identifying at-risk
  students early in a term using Google Classroom data. Teachers work through 22
  trigger indicators across three categories — platform engagement, academic
  performance, and behavioral/engagement signals — to generate a total risk
  score. That score maps to one of four action tiers (On Track, Monitor, Early
  Intervention, Urgent), each with a specific escalation protocol. The checklist
  also includes a Teacher Action Log for documenting outreach and a Supervisor
  Review & Sign-Off section, creating a complete, auditable intervention trail
  from first warning sign to resolution. Applicable to both young learners and
  adult learners, with context tags distinguishing where triggers apply.
problem: Struggling students are often flagged too late — after several weeks of
  silent disengagement have already compounded into failing grades or
  withdrawal. Teachers frequently rely on gut instinct rather than a consistent,
  defensible set of criteria, which leads to inconsistent early intervention,
  unclear escalation paths, and no paper trail if a student's situation needs to
  go to a supervisor or coordinator. This checklist solves that by converting
  vague concern ("this student seems off") into a quantifiable, standardized
  score, paired with a built-in documentation and sign-off workflow so nothing
  falls through the cracks and every intervention step is recorded.
outcome: >-
  Systematically screen every student against 22 concrete risk triggers across
  engagement, performance, and behavior

  Convert observations into an objective risk score (0–22) rather than subjective judgment

  Assign a clear risk tier (On Track / Monitor / Early Intervention / Urgent) with a matching response protocol

  Document specific outreach actions taken (private comments, calls, deadline extensions, parent contact, etc.) and track student response time

  Escalate cases to a supervisor with a full record of triggers, actions, and outcomes

  Close the loop with formal supervisor review, recommendations, and sign-off

  Catch disengagement early enough to intervene before it becomes academic failure or withdrawal
