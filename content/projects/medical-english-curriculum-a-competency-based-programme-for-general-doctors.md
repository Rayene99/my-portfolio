tagline: 40 sessions, one clinical voice — from first introduction to breaking
  bad news, fully in English.
summary: A 40-session, competency-based English for Medical Purposes (EMP)
  curriculum designed for general medical doctors at B1–B2 CEFR levels,
  delivered by English language teachers with no medical background required.
  The programme progresses across 8 units — from medical foundations and anatomy
  vocabulary, through consultation skills (history-taking, symptoms,
  examination), to diagnosis and treatment communication, clinical documentation
  (SOAP notes, referral letters, discharge summaries), interprofessional
  communication (handovers, case presentations, MDT meetings), and finally
  complex/sensitive communication (breaking bad news via SPIKES, informed
  consent, cross-cultural communication, ethics). Each of the 40 sessions
  includes CEFR level, competency focus, learning objectives, learning outcomes,
  and key language/vocabulary as a ready-to-teach planning reference.
problem: "Doctors are highly skilled clinicians but often lack the specific
  English needed to safely and confidently communicate in English-speaking
  clinical environments — with patients, families, and colleagues — while most
  English teachers assigned to teach them have no medical background to draw on.
  General ESL materials don't cover clinical registers, medical documentation
  conventions, or the emotionally complex communication (breaking bad news,
  consent, cultural sensitivity) that real practice demands. This curriculum
  solves both sides of that gap: it gives non-medical English teachers a fully
  scaffolded, session-by-session syllabus with the exact vocabulary, structures,
  objectives, and outcomes needed, so they can teach medically accurate,
  clinically relevant English without needing clinical training themselves."
outcome: >-
  Conduct a complete patient consultation in English — history taking,
  examination guidance, diagnosis delivery, and treatment planning

  Produce accurate clinical documentation (SOAP notes, referral letters, discharge summaries, case reports)

  Communicate effectively with colleagues via verbal handovers, case presentations, and MDT meetings

  Navigate sensitive clinical scenarios including breaking bad news, informed consent, and patient distress

  Read and discuss medical literature using evidence-based language

  Adapt communication style across audiences (patients, colleagues, superiors), settings (telehealth, emergency, outpatient), and cultural backgrounds

  Use accurate medical English grammar, vocabulary, pronunciation, and written conventions at a B2 level
