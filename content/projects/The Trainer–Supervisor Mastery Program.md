tagline: A four-month, 16-week backward-designed curriculum that turns
  experienced teachers into confident, ethical trainer-supervisors.
summary: A complete professional development curriculum for teacher trainers and
  supervisors, built on Understanding by Design and grounded in andragogy,
  constructivism, and reflective practice. It takes participants from
  foundational identity and needs-analysis work through training delivery and
  feedback craft, supervision and observation models, and finally coaching,
  mentoring, and institutional leadership, culminating in a portfolio and
  capstone demonstration.
problem: Trainer-supervisors are too often experienced teachers handed a new
  title with no deliberate preparation for the distinct skills the role demands
  — observing without prematurely evaluating, asking rather than telling, giving
  feedback that builds rather than diminishes, and designing training that
  respects adult professionals' intelligence and experience.
approach: Designed the entire 16-week program backward from five exit competency
  domains using Wiggins & McTighe's Understanding by Design, sequenced
  individual sessions with Gagné's Nine Events of Instruction, and grounded
  every design decision in andragogy, social constructivism, and Schön's
  reflective practitioner model. The curriculum moves through four integrated
  months — foundations and identity, training methods and feedback, supervision
  and observation, and coaching, leadership, and practicum — each pairing theory
  with hands-on practice tasks, common challenges, and trainer tips.
outcome: A fully specified, ready-to-deliver curriculum comprising 16 weekly
  session plans, a five-domain competency framework, a consolidated master
  syllabus, a complete portfolio-based assessment architecture with rubrics, and
  a set of professional templates (session planning, observation, conferencing,
  and growth planning) that facilitators can use to run the program end to end.
