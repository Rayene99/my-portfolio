tagline: Real-world English fluency for adult learners — from digital life to
  the boardroom to the departure gate.
summary: "A fully designed 10-week, 60-hour communication curriculum for adult
  English learners at A2–B1 level, built around five real-world thematic units:
  digital life, professional communication, lifestyle and wellbeing, travel, and
  critical expression. The programme comprises 30 complete, standalone 2-hour
  session plans (each following a consistent Warm-Up → Presentation → Guided
  Practice → Free Practice → Wrap-Up structure), full unit overviews, and a
  detailed end-of-programme assessment checklist covering curriculum alignment,
  instructional quality, learner progress, and evidence collection."
problem: 'Most adult EFL materials either drill isolated grammar points with
  little real-world payoff, or assume learners want "general English" without a
  clear communicative destination. Adult learners specifically need to function
  in adult contexts — job interviews, professional emails, travel logistics,
  opinion-giving, cross-cultural workplace communication — and they need it
  fast, with visible, motivating progress. This curriculum solves the gap
  between grammar-focused coursebooks and true communicative competence by
  anchoring every session in an authentic adult task (writing a LinkedIn
  summary, handling a hotel check-in problem, debating a real issue) rather than
  abstract language practice. It also solves the "curriculum in my head" problem
  for instructors: everything — objectives, materials, timing, teacher notes,
  sensitivity guidance — is fully scripted and ready to teach or hand off.'
outcome: >-
  Hold extended, natural conversations on familiar and semi-formal topics with
  reduced hesitation

  Write purpose-driven texts (emails, reviews, bios, recommendations) matched to audience and register

  Understand spoken and written English on everyday adult topics

  Apply functional language for describing, narrating, recommending, arguing, and presenting

  Adjust tone and register appropriately across digital, professional, and intercultural contexts

  Deliver a structured short presentation and participate confidently in debate/discussion
