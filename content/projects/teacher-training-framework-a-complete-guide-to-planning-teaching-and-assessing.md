tagline: From lesson plan to real connection — everything a teacher needs to
  plan, deliver, and assess contextualised online lessons with confidence.
summary: "A comprehensive training manual for novice and experienced online
  language teachers, covering the full lesson cycle: understanding the
  curriculum's contextualised, dialogue-based philosophy; conducting needs
  analysis and learner profiling; setting outcomes using Bloom's Taxonomy;
  sequencing lessons with the PPP framework (Presentation, Practice,
  Production); delivering each lesson phase (warmup through pronunciation
  practice); adapting strategies by age (young learners, teens, adults) and
  level (beginner through advanced); scaffolding and fading support; formative
  and summative assessment; error correction and feedback techniques; and best
  practices specific to the online classroom. It closes with a candid \"real
  teaching wisdom\" section on reading the room, managing energy, TTT reduction,
  and teacher self-care."
problem: New and experienced teachers often lack a single, structured reference
  that connects why a curriculum is designed a certain way to how to actually
  deliver it day-to-day — especially in an online setting, where pacing,
  engagement, and error correction all require different tactics than in-person
  teaching. Without this framework, teachers risk stripping context from
  lessons, misjudging learner needs, over- or under-scaffolding, giving vague
  feedback, or losing student engagement in the flatter online medium. This
  guide consolidates curriculum philosophy, lesson mechanics, age/level-specific
  strategy, assessment technique, and troubleshooting into one workflow-ordered
  resource, so teachers can make sound in-the-moment decisions instead of
  guessing.
outcome: >-
  Explain and preserve the curriculum's contextualisation principle rather than
  defaulting to abstract grammar teaching

  Conduct a needs analysis and build a learner profile to tailor instruction

  Write specific, observable learning outcomes using Bloom's Taxonomy

  Sequence and pace a lesson through the PPP structure (Presentation → Practice → Production) with appropriate timing for online delivery

  Deliver each lesson phase (warmup, theme intro, dialogue, practice, pronunciation) with clear technique

  Apply age- and level-specific strategies for young learners, teens, and adults across proficiency levels

  Scaffold effectively and fade support using the gradual release model (I do / we do / you do)

  Use formative and summative assessment, CCQs/ICQs, and structured error-correction techniques

  Give specific, actionable feedback instead of generic praise

  Troubleshoot common engagement, technical, and classroom-management issues in real time

  Read the room, manage personal energy, reduce Teacher Talking Time, and sustain their own wellbeing over time
