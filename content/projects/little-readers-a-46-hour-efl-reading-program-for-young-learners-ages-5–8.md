tagline: "From first page turn to first story retold: A complete
  phonics-to-comprehension pathway for EFL learners aged 5–8."
summary: A structured 46-hour EFL reading curriculum built for
  non-English-speaking classroom contexts, taking learners from zero print
  awareness through emergent decoding to independent story comprehension. The
  program is organized into six sequential units — Print Awareness, Phonological
  & Phonemic Awareness, Systematic Phonics, Sight Words, Reading Fluency, and
  Reading Comprehension — each with full session plans, learning outcomes mapped
  to Bloom's Taxonomy, and embedded formative and summative assessments. The
  design integrates seven instructional frameworks (ADDIE, Bloom's, UDL,
  Vygotskian scaffolding, Science of Reading, Multiple Intelligences, and
  TPR/play-based learning), giving it both pedagogical rigor and classroom
  practicality. Includes three fully scripted sample sessions, a complete
  assessment framework, differentiation strategies for mixed-age and
  mixed-ability groups, and a Kirkpatrick four-level program evaluation plan.
problem: "Most commercial EFL reading materials for young learners are either
  too generic (imported L1 phonics programs with no EFL-specific scaffolding) or
  too shallow (activity packs with no coherent scope, sequence, or assessment
  logic). Institutions adopting these materials often end up with disconnected
  worksheets rather than a real instructional pathway — and teachers are left
  improvising pacing, differentiation, and progress tracking on their own.
  LITTLE READERS solves this by providing an evidence-based, fully sequenced
  program purpose-built for non-native English classrooms: it accounts for zero
  oral English proficiency at entry, mixed L1 literacy levels, short attention
  spans, and the specific transfer challenges of learners coming from non-Latin
  scripts — while giving institutions a defensible, research-grounded curriculum
  they can point to for accreditation, parent communication, and quality
  assurance."
outcome: >-
  Demonstrate core print concepts (directionality, book parts,
  word/letter/sentence distinction)

  Manipulate spoken language at the phoneme level (rhyme, syllable, blend, segment)

  Produce all 26 letter-sounds and decode CVC words independently

  Instantly recognize 30–40 high-frequency sight words

  Read simple decodable texts with accuracy, pace, and expression

  Answer literal comprehension questions and retell a story in sequence

  Demonstrate sustained positive engagement with English reading, in class and at home
