tagline: Where language comes alive through story — 16 weeks, one magical world,
  four kids who grow right alongside your students.
summary: The Land of Luminos is a 16-week, 48-session English curriculum for
  young A1 beginners, built around a single continuous story rather than
  isolated units. Students follow Mira and her four friends through four
  narrative arcs — arriving in a new land, solving a garden mystery, rebuilding
  a broken bridge, and preparing for a festival — each session following a
  consistent Warm-Up → Story Time → Language Focus → Soft Skills Activity →
  Guided Practice → Cool Down structure. Along the way students acquire roughly
  250–300 A1 vocabulary items, core grammar (present/past/future tenses,
  prepositions, connectors), and nine soft skills (empathy, critical thinking,
  problem-solving, resilience, teamwork, conflict resolution, creativity,
  self-confidence, gratitude/reflection), assessed through nine embedded project
  sessions and a full teacher assessment toolkit.
problem: 'Most A1 curricula teach vocabulary and grammar in isolated,
  disconnected units, which gives young learners little intrinsic reason to
  actually use the language — and leaves soft-skill development (empathy,
  resilience, teamwork, conflict resolution) as an afterthought bolted onto
  language instruction rather than woven into it. Teachers are often left
  improvising how to make abstract language points feel meaningful, or running
  separate "SEL" activities disconnected from the language lesson itself. This
  curriculum solves both problems at once: it gives language a reason to exist
  (a character needs to describe something, solve something, resolve a conflict)
  so vocabulary and grammar are acquired in service of a story students are
  invested in, and it makes soft-skills growth measurable and inseparable from
  the language objectives rather than supplementary.'
outcome: >-
  Hold a simple, meaningful conversation in English on everyday topics

  Write a 5–6 sentence paragraph with a clear topic and supporting details

  Read and respond to short A1-level illustrated or written texts

  Present themselves and their ideas to a group with confidence

  Show measurable growth in at least 5 of 9 tracked soft skills (empathy, critical thinking, problem-solving, resilience, teamwork, conflict resolution, creativity, self-confidence, gratitude/reflection)

  Connect story events and characters to real-life experiences

  Participate actively and respectfully across pair, group, and whole-class activities

  Produce a complete portfolio of creative and reflective work spanning all four chapters, culminating in a final graduation speech and showcase
