tagline: 16 weeks. 4 skills. One confident young learner.
summary: A complete, ready-to-teach 16-week EFL curriculum for young learners
  (ages 8–13) at the A2 CEFR level. It includes 64 fully scripted two-hour
  sessions (128 instructional hours) organized into 16 thematic units — from
  "Who Am I?" to a final inquiry showcase — each with objectives, expected
  outcomes, materials lists, and minute-by-minute teaching procedures. Six
  formally assessed project sessions and a four-checkpoint teacher assessment
  checklist (covering listening, speaking, reading, writing, vocabulary,
  grammar, inquiry skills, and learner engagement) are built in, so the
  programme is classroom-ready with no additional lesson planning required.
problem: "Many EFL teachers of young learners face three recurring pain points:
  (1) grammar and vocabulary taught in isolated, decontextualized drills that
  fail to build real communicative competence; (2) a lack of session-by-session
  lesson plans that give clear, minute-timed guidance, forcing teachers to build
  lessons from scratch every week; and (3) no structured, embedded system for
  tracking student progress over an extended programme. This curriculum solves
  all three by anchoring every grammar/vocabulary point in a meaningful thematic
  context, scripting all 64 sessions in full teaching detail, and embedding six
  graded projects plus a four-checkpoint assessment rubric across the 16 weeks."
outcome: >-
  Use core A2 grammar (present/past simple, present continuous,
  comparatives/superlatives, modals, there is/are, have got, basic conditionals)
  accurately in speech and writing

  Command 300+ vocabulary items across 16 thematic units

  Read and comprehend short A2-level texts and answer questions on them

  Write structured sentences, paragraphs, and short texts on familiar topics

  Speak with growing confidence in conversations, discussions, and 2–3 minute presentations

  Formulate their own inquiry questions and present research findings using structured language

  Complete 6 assessed projects demonstrating holistic, real-world language use

  Reach demonstrable A2 competency across all four skills, ready to transition to A2+/B1
